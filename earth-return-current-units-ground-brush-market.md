<p><p>Earth Return Current Units, often referred to as Ground Brushes, are essential components in electrical systems, providing a path for current to return to the ground. The market for these units can be categorized into two main types: axial and radial. Axial markets involve configurations where the current flows parallel to the axis, typically in linear systems. In contrast, radial markets feature a flow that radiates outward from a central point, often seen in circular or distributed setups. Both types are vital for efficient and safe electrical operation.</p></p>
