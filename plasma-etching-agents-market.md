Wet etching agents use liquid chemicals to remove material from substrates, often providing uniform etching and being cost-effective for certain applications. In contrast, dry etching agents employ gas-phase chemicals, offering greater precision and control, essential for intricate patterns in semiconductor manufacturing. Each type serves distinct processes in industries like electronics, MEMS, and microfabrication, catering to varying material properties and operational requirements.</p></p>
