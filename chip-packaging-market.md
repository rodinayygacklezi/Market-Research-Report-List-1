<p><p>Chip packaging refers to the various methods used to encase semiconductor chips for protection and functionality. Traditional packaging typically includes approaches like dual in-line packages (DIP) and surface-mount devices (SMD), focusing on cost-effectiveness and standard functionalities. In contrast, advanced packaging encompasses techniques like system-in-package (SiP) and 3D packaging, which enhance performance, miniaturization, and integration. This market differentiation addresses the growing demands for higher efficiency, reduced footprint, and advanced connectivity in modern electronic applications.</p></p>
