<p><p>The lithium-ion battery active materials market comprises several key components. Cathode materials, such as lithium cobalt oxide and lithium iron phosphate, store and release lithium ions during charging and discharging. Anode materials, primarily graphite, facilitate lithium ion movement and contribute to energy storage. Electrolytes, which can be liquid or solid, enable the flow of ions between the cathode and anode. Additionally, other materials include additives and binders that enhance battery performance, stability, and lifespan, supporting diverse applications in consumer electronics and electric vehicles.</p></p>
