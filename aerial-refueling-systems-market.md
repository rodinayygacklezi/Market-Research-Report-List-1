<p><ul><li>Probe and Drogue</li><li>Boom and Receptacle</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The **Probe and Drogue** aerial refueling system uses a flexible hose with a stabilizing drogue at the end, which the receiver aircraft's probe connects to. It is versatile and commonly used by naval and smaller aircraft. The **Boom and Receptacle** system employs a rigid, telescopic boom controlled by an operator to connect to a receptacle on the receiver aircraft, offering higher fuel transfer rates, typically used by larger military aircraft like tankers and bombers. Both systems enhance operational range and endurance.</p></p>
