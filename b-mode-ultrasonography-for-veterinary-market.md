<p><p>B-mode ultrasonography in the veterinary market involves imaging techniques that utilize sound waves to create visual representations of internal structures in animals. The two-dimensional ultrasound provides flat images that help in diagnosing conditions. Three-dimensional (3D) and four-dimensional (4D) ultrasounds offer enhanced visualization, allowing for dynamic imaging, useful in prenatal assessments. Doppler ultrasound measures blood flow and circulatory dynamics, aiding in the diagnosis of cardiovascular issues. Collectively, these modalities enhance diagnostic accuracy and treatment planning in veterinary medicine.</p></p>
