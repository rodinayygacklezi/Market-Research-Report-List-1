<p><p>The cast iron cookware market can be categorized into two primary types: rounded and flat. Rounded cookware refers to items like Dutch ovens and soup pots, featuring curved sides that enhance heat distribution and moisture retention. These are ideal for stews and baking. In contrast, flat cookware encompasses skillets and griddles, with a smooth surface designed for frying and searing, allowing for rapid heat transfer. Both types are highly valued for their durability and ability to retain heat, appealing to various cooking styles.</p></p>
