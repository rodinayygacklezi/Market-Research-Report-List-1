<p><p>The X-Ray Crystallography Instruments market comprises two primary types: X-Ray Powder Diffraction (XRD) and Single-Crystal XRD. X-Ray Powder Diffraction is used to analyze the structural properties of polycrystalline materials by measuring the diffraction patterns of powdered samples. In contrast, Single-Crystal XRD targets single crystals to provide precise details about their atomic arrangement. Both techniques are essential for materials characterization, facilitating advancements in fields such as chemistry, physics, and materials science through their ability to reveal intricate molecular structures.</p></p>
