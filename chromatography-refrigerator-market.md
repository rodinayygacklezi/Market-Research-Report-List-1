<p><p>The chromatography refrigerator market includes low-temperature and ultra-low-temperature types, catering to different research and industrial needs. Low-temperature chromatography refrigerators typically operate between -10°C and -40°C, suitable for preserving samples and solvents used in various chromatographic processes. In contrast, ultra-low-temperature chromatography refrigerators function at temperatures below -40°C, often reaching -80°C or lower, making them ideal for storing sensitive biological samples and reagents that require more stringent temperature control to maintain stability and integrity.</p></p>
