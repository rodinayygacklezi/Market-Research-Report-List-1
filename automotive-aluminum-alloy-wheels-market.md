<p><p>The automotive aluminum alloy wheels market is primarily segmented into three types: casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds to create complex designs, providing a cost-effective manufacturing method for mass production. Forging, on the other hand, shapes heated aluminum under pressure, resulting in stronger and lighter wheels with improved performance characteristics. The "other" category includes processes such as extrusion and machining, which may be used for specialized applications or custom designs, offering unique performance advantages.</p></p>
