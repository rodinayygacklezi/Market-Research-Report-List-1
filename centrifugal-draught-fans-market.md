<p><p>Centrifugal draught fans are categorized into three main types based on their fan blade design: radial, backward, and forward. Radial fans feature blades that extend outwards from the center, providing high pressure and efficiency for a wide range of applications. Backward fans have blades angled backward, producing lower turbulence and higher efficiency, ideal for handling gases with dust or moisture. Forward fans possess blades that curve forward, delivering high airflow at low static pressure, making them suitable for general ventilation purposes.</p></p>
