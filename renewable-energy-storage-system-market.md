<p><p>Renewable Energy Storage Systems (RESS) can be classified into three main types: mechanical, electrochemical, and thermal. Mechanical storage includes systems like pumped hydro storage and flywheels, which store energy through physical movement. Electrochemical storage primarily involves batteries, including lithium-ion and flow batteries, which convert and store energy chemically. Thermal storage captures excess heat or cold for later use, typically through systems like molten salt or ice storage. Each type plays a crucial role in enhancing energy reliability and sustainability.</p></p>
