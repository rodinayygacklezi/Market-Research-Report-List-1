<p><ul><li>Threaded Fasteners</li><li>Non-Threaded Fasteners</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive bolt and fastener market includes threaded and non-threaded types. Threaded fasteners, such as bolts, screws, and studs, feature helical ridges for secure assembly and disassembly, ideal for high-stress applications like engine and chassis components. Non-threaded fasteners, including pins, clips, and rivets, rely on friction or mechanical locking for quick, permanent, or semi-permanent connections, often used in interior panels or lightweight assemblies. Both types ensure vehicle safety, durability, and performance, catering to diverse automotive manufacturing needs.</p></p>
