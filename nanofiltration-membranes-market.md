<p><p>Nanofiltration membranes are advanced filtration systems used for selective separation, purification, and concentration processes. They are categorized into three main types: Polymeric, Ceramic, and Hybrid. Polymeric membranes, made from organic polymers, are cost-effective and widely used for water treatment and desalination. Ceramic membranes, composed of inorganic materials, offer high thermal and chemical stability, ideal for harsh environments. Hybrid membranes combine organic and inorganic materials, balancing durability and efficiency, making them suitable for specialized applications requiring enhanced performance and adaptability across industries.</p></p>
