<p><p>Backpacking meals can be categorized primarily into raw and pre-cooked markets. The raw market includes dehydrated or freeze-dried ingredients that require water and cooking to prepare, offering lightweight, shelf-stable options ideal for long expeditions. In contrast, the pre-cooked market consists of ready-to-eat meals that only need heating, providing convenience for backpackers with limited time or cooking facilities. Both types cater to varying preferences for nutrition, preparation time, and packing ease, thereby enhancing the outdoor dining experience.</p></p>
