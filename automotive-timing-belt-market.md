<p><p>The automotive timing belt market is primarily divided into two types: rubber belts and metal chains. Rubber belts, commonly made from reinforced synthetic rubber, are lightweight, flexible, and relatively quiet, making them popular in many passenger vehicles. They require regular replacement due to wear. In contrast, metal chains, more durable and often used in high-performance applications, offer longer service life and handle greater loads, but can be noisier and heavier. Both types serve the critical function of synchronizing engine components.</p></p>
