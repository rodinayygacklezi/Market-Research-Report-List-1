<p><p>Expansion thermometers measure temperature by utilizing the expansion of liquids or gases in response to heat. </p><p>Liquid expansion thermometers, commonly filled with mercury or colored alcohol, expand within a calibrated tube, providing temperature readings based on liquid movement. </p><p>In contrast, gas expansion thermometers use gases that expand with temperature variations in a sealed container, translating gas pressure changes into temperature readings. Each type offers distinct advantages, such as precision and range, catering to various applications in industrial and scientific fields.</p></p>
