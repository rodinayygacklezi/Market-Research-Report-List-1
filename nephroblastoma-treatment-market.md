<p><p>The nephroblastoma treatment market comprises various therapeutic agents used in chemotherapy for Wilms tumor. Dactinomycin is a key drug that inhibits RNA synthesis, often used in initial treatment. Doxorubicin is an anthracycline that disrupts DNA replication and is effective in advanced cases. Vincristine works by preventing cell division, while Cyclophosphamide is an alkylating agent that interferes with DNA, reducing tumor growth. Etoposide targets topoisomerase II, and Irinotecan inhibits topoisomerase I, both playing roles in combination therapies for optimizing patient outcomes.</p></p>
