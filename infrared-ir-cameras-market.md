<p><p>The infrared (IR) cameras market is primarily divided into two types: cooled and uncooled cameras. Cooled IR cameras use cryogenically cooled sensors to enhance sensitivity and detect faint thermal signatures, making them ideal for high-end applications like military and scientific research. Uncooled IR cameras operate at ambient temperatures, using thermal detectors that require minimal power, making them more affordable and suitable for consumer and commercial applications, such as building inspections and surveillance. Both types serve distinct needs across various industries.</p></p>
